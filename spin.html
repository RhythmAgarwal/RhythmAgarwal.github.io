Spin, CHI'16

Design for accessisbility






PROBLEM

For wheelchair users finding accessible routes and destinations is a common problem that makes going out and participating in society a challenge. This problem is further complicated by the diversity of wheelchair users, which affects their abilities, limitations and use of assistive devices.
Currently there are few systems that provide accurate accessible routing services or location centric accessibility information to wheelchair users.




About Spin

Spin is a convenience-time-cost balanced, context-aware, personalized mobile application for wheelchair users to have reliable, easy and stress-free local trips.





SPIN APP

Planning a trip

With Spin, users can find a route solution that balances cost, time and comfort. Users can also customize their preference of transportation, to choose among public transportation, cabs and accessible ride-sharing services.

Alternative Recommendations

With Spin, users will be informed if their destinations are inaccessible for them. Meanwhile, Spin will provide them with other similar places accessible for them around their original destinations.

Feedback System

With Spin, during the trip if a user spots an issue, they can quickly snap a picture to be shared with other users. This geotagged photo is used to report the issue to be fixed by the city.

PROCESS

RESEARCH AND UNDERSTANDING

In order to understand the specific issues wheelchair users encounter in daily life, we conducted interviews with 12 wheelchair users with different limitations and are different kinds of manual and power wheelchairs.
We interviewed five experts, including one rehabilitation scientist, a Vocational Rehabilitation Counselor, two researchers and engineers on wheelchair and robotics, and one rehab clinician. Our interactions gave us an idea of the physical and mental difficulties of our target users.



Key Findings

- Complicated and time consuming trip
   planning process
- Lack of customization
- Unreliable Services

Design Visioning

Based on our findings, we came up with more than 20 initial design ideas and narrowed them down to 4 major design ideas.We evaluated these ideas by generating their pluses and minuses. Meanwhile, we thought about technology that is readily accessible to our wheelchair users. After evaluation, we decided to focus on designing service for planning a trip while handling uncertainty.

Storyboard and Evaluation

We tried out different ways of presenting accessibility information for trips to users. We presented our ideas at the Students for Disability Advocacy Disability Group at the University of Pittsburgh and The Spinal Cord Injury Peer Support group at UPMC Mercy Hospital.
