    LUTRON / Ra2 Select

    Designing a smart home control system




    About Ra2 Select

    Ra2 Select is a personalized smart home control system. With Ra2 Select you can intuitively control your lighting, shades, thermostat and other smart home devices and be in control of your home from anywhere.

    The new product was designed for homeowners who get their smart home set up through Electrical Contractors. It aims at improving the smart home installation process for contractors(electrical) and empowers homeowners with an intuitive control of their home.




    PROBLEM

    Before Ra2 Select

    • Lutron’s time taking process of setting up a system did not match contractor’s fast paced job process. Long hours invested in a required training and complicated system programming were major roadblocks for contractors in installing more of Lutron smart home systems.

    • Because sometimes contractor would cut corners to save time. Keypads were left blank and button scenes didn’t necessarily meet Homeowner’s needs. Homeowners were left with an unintuitive lighting experience.

    • Lutron’s mobile app was designed for maximum of 50 devices which faced problems of scalability and accessibility when the system was expanded over the limit.

    HOW MIGHT WE

    • Simplify the setup process for the contractor.
    • Give Homeowners an intuitive smart home lighting experience that fits their lifestyle.
    • Redesign the mobile app for scalability, findability and delight.

    SOLUTION

    Out of box scene programming

    To complement Contractor’s fast paced job and save them time, we used our data set and expert help to create out of box programming. Contractors without training can setup the system completely from a mobile app and programming is done automatically. Contractors still have the ability to tweak it and give it their personal touch.
    As an example, once Contractor has added lights to rooms, all lights in the home are automatically added and set to ‘off’ for the Away button on the Home/Away keypad. This saves the Contractor time from manually programming necessary scenes.

    Pre-engraved keypads

    We learned in our research how daily activities are a quintessential part of Homeowner’s life. With RA2 Select, we created pre-engraved Pico keypads that have buttons of homeowners most frequent activities.
    For example, the Home/Away keypad can be placed at the entry of the home. When the Homeowners is leaving, they can simply tap the away button to turn off all the lights. The Homeowner can relate to these activities, which makes selling easier for the Contractor.
    Upon learning through our research that checking the status of their home when they are away is of prime importance to homeowners, we introduced ‘Status’ in the app. We continuously iterated, got stakeholder feedback and then ultimately usability testing to arrive at the final design.

    Mobile App Redesign

    We had to rethink the information architecture of the app to account for more scalable layouts. Because Ra2 Select system could support devices too, we introduced room structure for better organization and findability of devices.

    We made a bunch of other updates too which I am going to talk about in the next section

    New Lutron Mobile App

    Tab navigation for easier accessibility

    Since usage of Scenes and Schedules were distinct high-intent less frequent usecases, we decided to put them behind tabs.

    Addressing information hierarchy issues

    By giving more real estate to the most common user activity- controlling individual devices, we wanted to address the issue with the large no of devices Ra2 could support. We decided to categorize devices by the area of the house they are in. Vertically stacking rooms and the devices under them helped us achieve better information hierarchy and made the layout more scalable.

    Status of devices

    With the growing number of devices, it was equally important to be able to see the state of all 'on' devices at one place- sort of like a 'Control Center'. This would help with homeowners' peace of mind and easy control.

    Visual Refresh

    With this new release, we wanted to give our app a visual refresh too. We believed this new look would resonate with our products' brand and quality. At the same time, it helped establish clarity and better visual hierarchy. This was our first step in starting to build and work with a design system.

    Motion Design(ONGOING)

    We have been working on crafting motion design and microinterations for critical screens in the app. We were focussed on primarily on reinforcing mental models of the UI to help the user better understand the information architecture and hence improve the usability and desirability. I went through multiple iterations and feedback to to achieve that. I cannot share the motion design work here as it hasn't shipped yet and it's under NDA.

    This was the first time the team was investing on motion design and I am glad that I got a chance to lead that effort. In an effort to evangalize and educate the team about motion design, we organized ‘UX in motion’ workshop by Issara Wiillenskomer for developers and designers to learn the tricks of the trade.

    PROCESS

    We were grounded in research and informed by data throughout the process. We interviewed 21 Contractors and 25 Homeowners. Synthesized the research to find significant patterns in the needs and motivations of our users. Created personas, journey maps and ‘a day in life’ to empathize with the pain points and
    understand users’ goals.

    We also analyzed the usage data from our current Residential systems, including Caseta, Radio Ra2 and HomeWorks. From the data, we tried to find pattern between the scenes, rooms and lights being used most often by the homeowners. I cannot share more about the process here because it's under NDA.
