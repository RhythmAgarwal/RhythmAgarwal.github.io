Google Design Exercise

Helping connect people looking for a new pet with the right companion for them












Problem

Millions of animals are currently in shelters and foster homes awaiting adoption.

Design an experience that will help connect people looking for a new pet with the right companion for them. Help an adopter find a pet which matches their lifestyle, considering factors including breed, gender, age, temperament, and health status.




RESEARCH

Research Questions

To understand the problem better I wanted answers to a few questions -

• Why are there millions of animals in shelters?
• What problems do adopters face in finding their match?
• What are adopters looking for in a right companion?
• What are the consequences of not finding a right companion?

Finding Answers

To scope down the exercise, I focused on dog adoption.

Secondary Research
To find the answers to this questions I did some secondary research and looked around for current solutions like Pet Finder and adoption shelter websites. Also looked at analogous domains like dating apps.

Dog Hike
I went on a dog hike with bunch (8-10) of adopters to talk to them and understand their relationship with their dogs and their journey of finding their companion.

Shelter Visit
I put myself in the shoes of an adopter and went to my nearby animal shelter looking to adopt a pet.




WHAT I HEARD

“I knew nothing about her. I saw a picture of her, that too a grainy picture of her and I wanted her. I went to the shelter and got her. I just got lucky , she is so well-behaved.”

“I got another dog thinking it would be a good company to my first dog but the other dog was too needy and my first dog didn’t get along with her. It was also getting too difficult for me to take care of two dogs. I had to rehome her.”

“It took me two months to find Siri, I kept looking on petfinder whenever I found time, tried to look up adoption events near me whenever possible. Once I found a dog that I really wanted till the time I contacted them it was gone”

“Most of the times, the behavior descriptions about pets online don’t seem so specific. It seems they all say the same thing honestly.”

KEY FINDINGS

1. While adopting from shelters or adoption events, lifestyle questions are asked much later in the process for approving a choice rather than making a match.

Since lifestyle approval is done in the end when adopter already has their heart set on a pet, there disappointments and compromises. And none of the current aggregator apps are asking lifestyle questions to find pet options. There is also lack of trust in quizzes that find adopter their match.


2. Currently, the only way to understand pet’s behavior and temperament is to go down to the shelter or adoption events and talk to pet’s caretaker which is time consuming.

Choosing a pet as a companion is an emotional decision highly driven by pet’s appearance and behaviors. People believe the most in caretaker’s assessment of pet’s behavior. It is time consuming and repetitive for both adopter and caretakers to talk about pet’s behavior to each individual.


3. Pets come and go at a shelter, events happen nearby for adoption and people do not get to know if they have missed a good match if they are not actively looking.

Adopters are making all the efforts to come across and meet their potential meets, there are no updates or pull from the shelters once adopters have left the shelters or the adoption events.





OPPORTUNITIES

1. We can reduce the disappointments and compromises by knowing about the lifestyle of an owner earlier in the process. And help them choose a companion which is right for them from an aggregate pool of potential pets.

2. In addition to good matchmaking, we can provide more meaningful and substantial ways to get to know the behavior and appearance of a potential pet better so that adopters don’t have to ask the caretaker about the behavior of every pet they are interested.

3. Once we know about the lifestyle and preferences of an adopter we can nudge potential adopters whenever there’s a good match near them so that they don’t miss any chance of being with their perfect companion.

SOLUTION

Learning about adopter’s lifestyle

Unlike the current process, Amigo will get to know about the adopter’s lifestyle before the adopter start browsing and to make a choice rather than approve one. This which will make it easier for adopters to find a right companion for them. I have taken examples of questions from current adoption forms but these lifestyle questions can be created with the help of shelter experts and more research.

Value proposition and how it works

During research, I found that people trust more in shelter assistants and pet caretakers than any online medium to know pet compatibility. Our matching algorithm should be built with the help of these experts and their knowledge.
In ‘How it works’, we should explain that we have taken inputs from shelter experts and caretakers to build the algorithm and gain adopters’ trust in the system.

Why are we asking the question and how is the answer related to compatibility

With each question, it would be helpful for the users to know why is the system asking this question and how would it help them find a right companion. With each choice of answer, we tell the user what behavior and characteristics in a pet the system would look for according to the answer. This would keep the adopters informed, gain trust in the system and increase involvement.



Preferred personality traits

In secondary research, I found that temperament is the second most important factor people are looking at while making a decision, after appearance. Adopters didn’t have desired traits on the top of their head, but when asked from a list they excitingly picked what they were looking for. A combination of both lifestyle fit and preferred traits would help adopters find their compatible and desired match.

First Look to pique interest

Adopters are shown their compatible matches labeled as - Great, good and fair matches. All the adoptable pets are listed, sorted by match. Only scannable information is displayed on cards to pique interest. Users can go into the detailed view to know more about the pet, it’s qualities and vulnerabilities, also about shelter, foster home or individual that it currently belongs to. Users can also filter this list down if they would like to get a dog with a specific attribute. Cards with videos, will have the video autoplay on mute when it comes into view to create an immersive and engaging discovery experience.



Detail view to dive deeper

While the first view makes adopters curious and interested in the dog, detailed view lets adopter take the next steps in the process. Adopters get to know how the dog matches with their lifestyle, they look at both, what’s good with pet’s health and what are the things to consider about their health. Being transparent is the key so that people set the right expectations. They can ask more specific questions to the shelter/foster/individuals here. Once people are interested and confident about compatibility, they are provided with shelter information so they can schedule a visit or walk-in to the shelter to look at their pet. Because there is nothing like looking at a pet in person.

Live Video to learn about temperament and behavior

Behavior, temperament and appearance of a dog are most important to consider when making decision about a compatible pet. Live video of the pet on walk with their volunteers, of socializing with other dogs of training or vet visits will give adopters a better idea of their personality and help them more feel connected with them before they go visit. These live videos are later saved as videos that people who missed the lives session can look at.
Adopters can also ask questions during the live session much like the live sessions today on other platforms.



Continuously learning algorithm

As adopters choose their favorites and spend more time engaging with the app, we can rightly channel their interests as signals that are then taken in to modify matches accordingly.

Notifications whenever a new 'great match' or nearby event is found

As found in insight #3, adopters may a times miss a match or an event because they weren’t actively looking every day. This adds time to the process of adoption. Notifications will help adopters meet their companion sooner leaving space for more pets in need. This would make sure users visit the app and are engaged through nudges in the process of finding their pet.

User Feedback

I looped back with people I talked to for research for their feedback. Things people liked-

People loved Live Videos.

“I like that in a live video with their caretaker I can see the dog in a more social and friendly environment. This brings out more of their personality and behavior compared to high stress environments in shelter kennels and adoption events.”

People liked getting nudges about matches and event

“Currently, information is scattered, it’s hard to find things until you are looking and you have to know where to look for. This is brings the information to you which will save time and effort”

People liked the temperament and behavior keywords used to describe pets.

“When describing the pet I want open-endedly, I am not sure if what I said was interpreted correctly so picking from a list helps. Usually, there is a paragraph written and I don’t get the gist of it. Keywords along with description makes me more confident and trust the traits of the dog.”



Suggestions from users-

“Ranking the traits by most important to least important might help in further sorting”

“Ability to change the lifestyle answers later will be helpful if the conditions of living changes etc.”

“If I am really interested in a dog, I can probably ask them to hold it for me?”

“It would also help to know stuff post adoption, tips on how to take care in the beginning for a new adopter”





Stretch Goals

Given more time and resources, I would have loved to look into creating a system that helps adopters even after the adoption in things like-

• Keeping track of vet visits
• Upcoming dog meet events like hiking or playing
• Suggested dog food and products personalized for their dog
• Finding a babysitter in times of need
• Finding places to hangout that are dog-friendly
• Being part of a community that loves to help each other with dog issues (medical or otherwise)
